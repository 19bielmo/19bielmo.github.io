Reflecting Better than a Mirror.

Players either found the flies, or they didn't. Regardless, they learned a lot about the Web.


I used a combination of HTML and CSS to make a game where people had to move from page to page, finding flies. It featured code for images, backgrounds, colors, and (in my opinion) some funny jokes.

One challenge I encountered was that I had difficulty adding customizations to my writing, in terms of mastering the right size, color, and style of my words. I think it was also difficult to coordinate all of the different web pages, with exact uniform spelling so that players can smoothly transition from file to file.

One element of my game that I am proud of is my coding to make the fly move as the person scrolls down the file. It was one of the advanced coding features, where I could make the fly move down the page, instead of being stationary, and it looked really good! I was also proud of my meme/pop culture references, but that doesn't have to do with coding.

I could extend my game by adding more current pop culture pages, such as the yodelling boy from Walmart, or maybe a spongebob meme. I could also give it a little more plot, so it resembled a traditional choose your own adventure story more.

I hope you enjoy, and find all of the flies lost in the Web!
